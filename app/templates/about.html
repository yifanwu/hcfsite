Current Projects

Harvard China Forum

The annual Harvard China Forum is North America's leading student forum regarding China, Harvard China Forum holds a conference each spring at Harvard University, focusing on opportunities and challenges that China faces. In the past, conference topics have included business, economics, finance, real estate, education, health, media, and non-profit organizations in China. The conferences has focused on themes like the Asian Financial Crisis (1998), Sino-US Relations (1999), China and the WTO (2001), Sustainable Growth (2002), China's Consumption Market (2005), PRC after Sixty (2010), and Century of Revolution (2011). Harvard China Forum facilitates discussion for academics, business leaders, government officials, and other leaders who are concerned about issues and opportunities in China. In addition, Harvard China Forum provides the annual platform for students from the worlds’ top universities and institutions to be updated regarding China’s trends.

 

HSTAGE

HSTAGE (HCCF Summer Taskforce for the Advancement of Global Exchange) allows students from all across the world interested in China to acquire valuable experiences. Every summer, HSTAGE takes some of the United State’s and China’s best university students to visit Chinese companies, government agencies, and non-governmental organizations and engage in challenging dialogue. In the past two years, top students from Harvard, Yale, Princeton, Columbia, the University of Pennsylvania, Peking University, Tsinghua University, Foreign Service Institute, Renmin University and other colleges have participated. Some of the venues visited include the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, the Ministry of Commerce, the Supreme Court, the Office of Diversion Project, Goldman Sachs China, McKinsey, Lenovo, Li Ning, Baidu, New Oriental, CNOOC, the World Bank, and the Social Entrepreneur Foundation.

HCF History

The Harvard Square stop on the Red Line of the MBTA subway system is across the street from the Harvard Yard.

From the Harvard Square stop on the Red Line
Main Subway exit
Take the two sets of escalators out of the T stop (this is the Main exit). After exiting, you will need to reverse direction and head up Massachusetts avenue (away from the newsstand and Information Kiosk). Continue walking past Cambridge Savings Bank, crossing Dunster St. (The Harvard gates should be on your left side) After the Au Bon Pain Café, turn to the right and enter the Holyoke Center, we are located inside the Holyoke Arcade.

From South of Boston: Drive to Braintree MBTA station parking garage (Braintree exit 17 off of Route 3 coming from Cape Cod or the South Shore). Take the Red Line in the direction of Alewife to “Harvard.” Allow approximately 45 minutes for the subway portion of the trip.

From the North of Boston: Drive to the Alewife MBTA station parking garage. Take the Red Line. Get off at the third stop, “Harvard.” Allow fifteen minutes for the subway portion of the trip.

From the West of Boston: Drive to Riverside MBTA station in Newton, the western terminus of one branch of the Green Line (Exit 22A from Route 128), where there is an open-air parking lot. Take the Green Line to the “Park Street” stop. Go downstairs, following signs marked “Red Line to Alewife.” Take train in the direction of Alewife four stops to “Harvard.” Allow one hour for the subway portion of the trip.
